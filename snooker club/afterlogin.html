Snooker Tables

Snooker is a popular cue sport that is played on a large rectangular table called a snooker table.

Dimensions:
A standard snooker table is typically 12 feet (3.66 meters) long and 6 feet (1.83 meters) wide.

Cushions:
The table is surrounded by cushions, which are made of rubber and covered with cloth.

Pockets:
Snooker tables have six pockets, one at each corner and one in the middle of each long side.

Markings:
The snooker table has several markings that help players during the game. The most important markings are the D and the spots.

Cloth:
The cloth covering the snooker table is traditionally green in color, although other colors such as blue are sometimes used.

Lighting:
Snooker tables are often illuminated by overhead lighting to ensure even and adequate lighting across the playing surface.
Good lighting is crucial to enable players to see the balls and accurately judge their shots.

Equipment:
Snooker tables are played with a set of 21 balls, including 15 red balls worth one point each, and six differently colored balls with different point values.
